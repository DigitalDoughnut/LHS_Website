Cookie Policy

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites place on the computers and mobile devices of people who visit those websites. These files are then read by the website each time you return to the site. These text files allow a website to remember your device and how you interacted with the website. This is useful for a number of different purposes, like letting you navigate between pages efficiently, remembering your preferences, and generally improving your experience on the website. For more information on the different types of cookies, see the links at the bottom of this policy.

How does this website use cookies?

This website uses one first party cookie (a cookie that has been placed and is used by this website). It is used to remember that you have accepted the use of cookies on this website. This cookie is anonymised, and it cannot track your browsing activity on other websites. No other party can access the data in this cookie.

This website also includes third party cookies (cookies that have been placed and are used by other organisations). For example, the Youtube video featured on this website contains cookies that are activated when you click to view it. The owner of this website cannot access the data in these cookies and does not control the setting of these cookies. For more information on the third party cookies used on this site and how to manage them, please refer to the Google cookie policy (Youtube). A link to their cookie policy can be found at the bottom of this policy.

This website does not use advertising cookies or analytical cookies to track your use of the site, but be aware that the third party cookies may do so.

By closing the pop-up or continuing to use the website you are agreeing to the use of cookies by this website.

Cookies in External Links

This website also contains links to third party sites that may use cookies, including if you click to subscribe to Lauren’s newsletter. The sign-up page is run by MailChimp. By clicking on the links to these third party sites, you are agreeing to their use of cookies. Please refer to their cookie policies as necessary. A link to the MailChimp cookie policy can be found at the bottom of this policy.

Disabling cookies

If you would like to restrict the use of first party cookies on this website, you can leave the pop-up active. Please be aware that this will not restrict the third party cookies activated if you click to watch the video. By doing so, you are agreeing to the use of third party cookies contained in the video. Alternatively, you can control the cookie settings on your Internet browser. For advice on how to do this, please use the software help (normally available via key F1). This will not restrict the use of cookies by third party sites with links from this site.

If you would like to restrict the use of cookies on the third party sites with links from this site, you can disable them as per the policies of those third party sites or through your browser’s cookie settings, as detailed above.
