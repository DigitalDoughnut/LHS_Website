The Legacy Chronicles

When I first decided to quit teaching to become a full time writer, I had ideas for more than one series of books. The first was based on my experiences in the classroom and would have covered my entire teaching career. The second was based on the stories of how various couples in my family met and fell in love.

As I began to talk through what I had in mind with my family, however, an entirely different story kept forcing its way to the front of my thoughts. A series based on the women in Moses’ life who displayed such inspiring acts of faith that they have always been amongst my favourite Biblical characters.

The concept grew to include my love of science-fiction and fantasy, fleshing out without much conscious input on my part. It felt like these stories wanted to be told and wouldn’t leave me alone until I’d written them, even waking me in the middle of the night with new details. I caved. I put aside my original plans and followed the call to write The Legacy Chronicles.

I can only hope that you enjoy reading them as much as I have enjoyed putting them to paper. The characters of the Esarelian Empire and beyond live in a time and place where choosing faith in God over scientific understanding and personal advancement is difficult and often dangerous. The results are… well, why don’t you find out for yourself?

Courage

Can one woman evade an alien empire to save her son’s life?

Gilla and Elias have the perfect life—or as perfect as life can be for slaves living deep in the Esarelian Empire. Elias wants freedom for his precious wife and daughter, and the rest of their people, and he is willing to fight to get it. Pregnant with her second child, Gilla only wants a happy, healthy family who share a strong faith in the God of Old Earth, and to get through her workload each day.

When the Esarelians decide to teach their rebellious slaves a lesson they will never forget, Gilla’s entire life is turned upside down. She must trust that God has a plan to protect her new-born son, and have the courage to follow it, choosing the strangest of allies in an effort to ensure his survival.

Conviction

Can two people with opposing principles overcome their differences to be together?

Than has spent his life ostensibly having fun while secretly fighting for his people’s freedom. A member of the underground resistance, he is only ever serious around his comrades and his family. When an injury forces him to step down from active duty and his reluctant nurse sparks his interest, Than finds himself in uncharted territory. The fascinating woman will have nothing to do with him…

Menali’s past has taught her to keep her head down and trust that God has a reason for allowing the human race to suffer on U’du. When Than explodes into her life, he refuses to take no for an answer and challenges all of her preconceptions. He soon has her re-evaluating her priorities and wondering what life with someone like him would be like…

Reemah is a Ra’hos. She has led a life of privilege and luxury, flouting the rules to suit her every whim. Now she must face the consequences of breaking those rules to save a human infant. What will her father demand in return for allowing her latest, most reprehensible, disobedience?

Chapter 1

Reemah stepped from the cargo bay of Buln, the transport tub she and Velay had requisitioned for their illicit trips to U’du, onto the solid deck of the cruiser. She stretched her back to remove the kinks caused by cycles in the pilot’s seat. Buln was possibly the least comfortable craft she had ever flown, but it was worth the discomfort to be able to see her adopted human son, Mahsan. Sighing in relief, both at the familiar, comforting environment and at having returned once more without detection, she strode out of the hangar in a random direction.

Velay chased after her, their gear slung over her shoulder in a small pack. She made a sound in her throat when they took the corridor leading to the exercise bay but caught up and strolled alongside Reemah without complaint. After a close call a few months ago, they had taken to wandering the cruiser as a cover for their clandestine excursions and never returned directly to their own suite.

Reemah mentally reviewed the highlights of their visit to the planet. She had been pleased at the progress Mahsan had made since she had last seen him four months ago. He was already speaking a few words and standing on his own, and soon he would be toddling around, getting into trouble. If only she could find a way to bring him aboard the cruiser, she would be able to see each accomplishment herself instead of relying on the reports of the human nursemaid and guard she had assigned to look after him. She missed him greatly.

Velay looked over at her and shook her head, evidently reading Reemah’s surface emotions. “Already, huh?” In an undertone, she added, “I guess he does grow on you after a while.”

Reemah chuckled at her friend’s admission but refrained from commenting directly. She knew how prickly a subject her adopting a human child still was for Velay, despite his adorable nature.

“I can’t believe how big he is now. I need to get him up here, Velay, as soon as possible. This is intolerable, and we’re going to get caught if we keep sneaking out to visit him.”

Reemah had been banned from space flight by her father, the Ra’hon of the Esarelian Empire, after an unfortunate incident during an illicit arrow race a year earlier. It was during her first stolen cycles away with Velay that she had found and adopted her son. She had instantly bonded with the boy, the only survivor of a culling of human infant males, and had vowed to protect him from further harm.

Unfortunately, she had yet to discover a means of officially claiming him without disclosing her disobedience or admitting to interfering with her father’s orders regarding their slave workforce on U’du. Still, whatever his reason – and she desperately hoped the father she adored had a good one for such a barbarous act, or that the order had originated elsewhere, unbeknownst to him – she could not condone the slaying of innocents. She would do all in her power to ensure Mahsan’s survival and prosperity as a member of her family. Glumly, she reminded herself that going against the Ra’hon was potentially suicidal, even for her, and that she had done little thus far to prove her intentions towards her new son.

“Don’t worry,” Velay replied, breaking into her musings. “We’ll think of something. We always do.”

That brought a smile to Reemah’s lips. Soon, my son, she vowed, soon.

Nishaf waited impatiently for the pilot of the small boat, a private pleasure craft, to disembark and make his way over. As soon as he was within two spans and out of hearing of the hangar crew, Nishaf barked, “Well?”

“Hon Nishaf.” The male made a half bow of greeting before answering further, the mark of respect cooling Nishaf’s impatience somewhat. “The tracking device worked; they never knew anyone was following them.”

A member of the underclasses, the young male had been in a gaming den when Nishaf had found him. After providing training in several rather specific areas, Nishaf had utilised his natural talents on numerous occasions over the last century. The male was of average appearance, able to blend in and move undetected in many circles, and his unscrupulous tendencies made him perfect for the work Nishaf could not assign to others within his employ. At his own request, the male had remained nameless and covert, but he was extremely loyal to Nishaf.

“And? Where did they go?” Nishaf demanded.

The male frowned as he answered, “To U’du, Hon Nishaf.”

Nishaf’s instincts immediately went on alert. The slave colony on that planet was primarily devoted to producing the highest grade uduin in the known universe, as well as manufacturing the newest space cruiser in the Esarelian fleet. As chief scientific advisor to the Ra’hon, Nishaf had overall responsibility for the work and did not relish an inquisitive Ra’hos poking around in his domain.

Keeping his features neutral, he asked, “U’du?” What in the universe are you up to, Reemah?

“That is the strange thing, Hon Nishaf. It sounded as if they were visiting humans.”

Nishaf narrowed his eyes and scrutinised the male to determine whether he was being honest or had lost them and was inventing a tale to take the pressure from his own back. If so, it was a preposterous one to choose. He waved for the underling to continue.

“I set down after they disembarked and had to remain on the other side of a small ridge to avoid detection. They were talking to a human male about a boy, asking how he had been since they last saw him.”

“Boy,” Nishaf interjected sharply. “What boy?” Not only were Reemah’s movements nonsensical, it seemed they could potentially prove disastrous to his plans.

“I do not know; they did not remain there for long. When I could no longer hear them, I moved closer, but they had disappeared. They must have entered one of the planet’s cave systems. I did not want to risk discovery by attempting to find the entrance, so I returned to report instead, Hon Nishaf.”

Nishaf ground his teeth at the missed opportunity; he craved more detailed information. While he would admit there were several such systems on the planet, some natural and others man-made, he could not countenance Reemah evading his spy. When she became his, he would control her far better than her father had until now.

Returning his attention to the male he had set to watch her, he debated whether or not to let the failure go unpunished. After all, the male was ruthlessly efficient and had remained undetected, qualities Nishaf prized in his employees. He had also discovered the destination of Reemah’s adventures on the tub.

“Never mind, you did well. Remember to speak of this to no-one, though, or you will find yourself on U’du permanently.”

The male’s jaw flexed infinitesimally, but he bowed and replied, “Of course, Hon Nishaf. You can be assured, as always, of my complete discretion.”

Nishaf dismissed him, already pondering his next move. Stalking towards the control station in the innermost corner of the bay, he beckoned the female on duty with a flick of his finger in her direction. She scurried over, pleasing him with her quick obsequious bow.

“Was that Ra’hos Reemah I just saw leaving this hangar?” Nishaf asked in his most imperial tone.

Her eyes widened and her mouth gaped for a moment before she rushed to answer, “Yes, Hon Nishaf.”

“Yet you failed to report her presence here to the Ra’hon.”

He did not need to say more. All control personnel knew of Reemah’s restrictions regarding travel beyond the cruiser. The young female could be severely disciplined for her lapse and knew it.

“A-apologies, Hon Nishaf,” she stuttered. “I will do so right away.”

Good, she knew to use his honorific each time she addressed him. He held up a finger to forestall her and studied her closely. She kept her face blank and her eyes downcast, but the twitching of her right hand as it clasped the left in front of her gave away her nerves. He could use her evident fear of him to his advantage.

Unfortunately, acting now would be a waste of time as well as being counterproductive to his plans. Reemah had already returned to the cruiser and would no doubt have tampered with the internal sensors to show her having never left. The word of a Ra’hos superseding all others, there would be no point in a useless confrontation that would only tip his hand. No. He needed proof of her perfidy before alerting her father.

“You need say nothing of her movements today now that I am aware. However, unless you wish to be found responsible for aiding her in circumventing the Ra’hon’s decree, you will immediately report to your superiors the next time she leaves this cruiser.”

“Yes, Hon Nishaf, of course.” Her full bow held slightly longer than necessary, causing his chest to swell with pride. His reputation among the ranks of the underclasses preceded him and none would risk disobeying his orders. As he swept out of the hangar, he congratulated himself on the morning’s work.

Who's Who

Gilla

I'd like you to meet Gilla Trestam, the main character in Courage. Here she is, as she appears at the start of the story. I never truly considered how wrong it seemed for Gilla to be doing manual labour while 7 months pregnant until I actually saw her in her uniform. It’s clear that something on planet U’du needs to change – and soon!

Name: Gilla Trestam.
Age: 30 at the start of the series.
Status: Married to Elias, one child (Mira, 7) and pregnant with her second.
Position: Slave worker in the manufacturing sector on planet U’du, proficient in wiring circuitry and systems testing.
Appearance: Average height (5ft 7in), athletic build, warm brown eyes, long dark-brown hair reaches to her waist.
Attributes: Calm, nurturing, peace keeper, intelligent, firm believer in Old Earth’s God, good singing voice.
Favourite Activity: Singing the songs of Old Earth, passed down by her grandmother, to her children.

Reemah

Let me also introduce Ra'hos Reemah, one of the Esarelian characters from Courage. Reemah fills the role of the Egyptian princess in the Biblical account of Moses, and more of her story is told in Strength, the third book in The Legacy Chronicles.

Name: Reemah, First Daughter of Ashal of Esarel.
Age: 256 at the start of the series.
Status: Single
Position: Ra’hos (member of the Esarelian royal family).
Appearance: Tall for a female (8ft approx.), thin, toned, violet eyes signal Ra’hos lineage.
Attributes: Rebellious, intelligent, easily bored, great pilot, craves space and speed, odd for an Esarelian – doesn’t like political games, feels stifled by her role, dislikes sycophants, often lonely.
Favourite expression: Solid!
Favourite activity: Racing arrows (military fighter craft).
